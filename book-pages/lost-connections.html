Lost Connections

Why You're Depressed and How to Find Hope

By Johann Hari ©2018

Author Johann Hari asserts that depression is a reasonable reaction to one's life circumstances, with logical resolutions that provide lasting relief.

About the Author

Johann Hari has been taking antidepressants since he was a teenager, and has long wondered if there may be more effective solutions.

Favorite Ideas

  • Pharmaceutical companies selectively publish studies that highlight antidepressants as effective. Unpublished studies suggest that improving sleep works better as an antidepressant than medications, which may have a placebo effect at best.
  • The DSM, a diagnosis tool, included a clause that said if a person is grieving, their signs of depression are a rational grief response rather than depression to be treated. This clause was removed. Could depression be rational responses to other life circumstances?
  • Studies show that people are likely to become depressed when they face a long term stressor or difficult situation without the social support they need.
  • Common causes of depression include feeling disconected from a meaningful occupation, other people, nature, and one's values. Childhood trauma and genetics also play a role.
  • In Cambodia, a citizen became depressed after being injured and unable to work. The village came together to give the guy a cow and he was able to become a farmer. He was eventually happier.
  • Hari suggests that effective antidepressants can be found in reconnections. Some actionable antidepressants:
    • Do something with others that lifts you up, preferably in nature. For example, you could plant a garden.
    • Pursue autonomous, meaningful work where you feel you make an impact and do a good job.
    • Process internal childhood trauma. A therapist can be helpful here.
    • Consider how your lifestyle upholds your values. Develop a positive vision for where you see yourself in 5 years.
