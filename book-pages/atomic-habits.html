Atomic Habits

Tiny Changes, Remarkable Results

By James Clear ©2018

The daily choices we make every day add up over time. James Clear explains how habits work and how to leverage them to build a life you love.

About the Author

Clear discovered the power of habits after being hit in the face by a baseball bat in high school. Through small daily decisions that compounded into long term success, Clear finished his college career earning accolades and honors for his academic and athletic performance. Clear is a bestselling author and keynote speaker.

Favorite Ideas

  • Doing something one time won't matter much inn the long term. However, the choices you make every day compound over time.
  • Winners and losers have the same goals. Goals represent where you want to go, and the system is how you get there. Design a system you love and identify with that will take you to your goals.
  • You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems.
  • Your systems must align with your identity. Instead of thinking "I want this", think "I am this". Continuing your habit provides evidence for your statement. While there is nothign wrong with indulgence every now and then, you won't see yourself as a healthy person if you eat cake every day.
  • Habits are a logical problem/solution situaiton with four stages. First, you experience a cue and a craving. You wish to resolve them, so you respond and receive a reward.
  • You can use habits to your advantage by:
    • Modifying your environment ton make desired cues obvious and undesired cues invisible
    • Make the habit something you want to do
    • Make your habit the easiest option if you want to encourage it, or add barriers to reduce the habit
    • Make sure you see the reward of your actions
  • Remember, your actions will compound over time if you stay diligent.
