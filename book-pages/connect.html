Favorite Ideas

  • Sleep is likely an evolutionary trait that supports our brains in creativity, intelligence, and emotional regulation. REM sleep is considered an evolutionary benefit that made humans the dominant species.
  • Whether you are a morning lark or a night owl, you have a natural circadian rhythm that determines your sleep cycle. Humans need about 7-9 hours of sleep each night. Melatonin can help adjust your body clock from jet lag, but will not improve sleep quality.
  • Sleep consists of 90 minute cycles of NREM and REM sleep. During NREM sleep, brain waves move in unison from the front of the brain to the back, moving memories from short term to long term storage. During REM sleep, the brain paralyzes the body and dreams occur
  • Some animals dream, and some sleep with half their brain. There is not a clear detemining factor for the amount of sleep a specific species needs. We are the only species that wakes up artificially.
  • Babies are sleep nearly the entire time they are in the womb. REM sleep is critical for development in the third trimester and for a while after birth. Sleeping in a hospital is difficult yet critical for patients, especially premature babies.
  • Children and elderly people have early circadian rhythms, while teenagers have theirs shifted forward much later. A lack of sleep may cause psychological disorders in teenagers and young adults, and dementia, alzheimers, among other illnesses in elders. Schools are not teaching the benefits of getting enough sleep, and and start too early for the natural teenage circadian rhythm. Students perform on average 200 SAT points better when high school start times are pushed forward 1 hour.
  • Adequate sleep provides proper management of short and long term memory, and improves learning outcomes.
  • Sleep deprived people are not aware of their impaired performance, which declines to that of a drunk driver after 19 hours awake. Every hour, someone dies in a car accident resulting from lack of sleep. Sleep deprivation erodes productivity in office workers and produces the 3rd leading cause of death - medical errors.
  • Reduced sleep weakens the immune system and causes faster aging. Sleep deficiency increases your chances of heart attack by 200%. It is a leading cause of type 2 diabetes. Rats die after approximately 20 days with no sleep.
  • Dreams heal emotional difficulties and solve problems. During dreaming, the visual, motor, emotional, and autobiographical portions of the brain become active. The prefrontal cortex becomes inactive and the brain paralyzes the body.
  • Lucid dreamers can control their dreams. Sleepwalking is a rare and usually harmless condition. Emotional worry or stress can cause insomnia.
  • While advancements in electricity and technology are beneficial for many reasons, innovations have negatively impacted sleep. A bedside lamp is powerful enough to alter your brain's sleep signals. Advancements in smart technology, like AI powered thermostats and lights, can be used to promote sleep.
  • Alcohol significantly impairs your sleep and dramatically decreases your memory abilities. Sleeping pills do not produce a natural sleep and have negative impacts on memory and health.
